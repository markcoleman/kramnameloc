Every year [Members 1st FCU](https://www.members1st.org) invites associates to bring their kid to work to learn about Members 1st FCU and participate in activities organized by associates.  Each associates that volunteers helps put together a little activity for the children highlighting a business unit.

 - Tele-branch
 - Security
 - Learn about being a Teller 
 - Data Center Tour
 - Computer lab activity (*That's Me*)

Over the past few years I did not participate for one reason or another (even though I should have looking back), but this year was the year.  While judging a [middle school computer fair](http://www.caiu.org/services/technology/computer-fair.aspx) there was an excellent presentation about the [Makey Makey](http://makeymakey.com) [(video)](https://vimeo.com/60307041) by an employee at [Ritter Insurance](https://www.ritterim.com) showing how you could make controllers with alligator clips and a computer.

The day after seeing that awesome presentation I brought the idea to the IT kids day group and thought it would be a good fit for the IT based activity.  The group began pondering and googling of potential activities.  We came up with four stations where the kids would assemble a makey makey and play with their creation.

##Mario Doh!
The first station was ran by [Jessica Hutchinson](https://www.linkedin.com/pub/jessica-hutchinson/27/a41/756) where the kids would create and assemble a video game controller using either Play Doh ® or drawing a controller using a thick pencil.
![Mario Doh!](https://silvrback.s3.amazonaws.com/uploads/6768dcbb-30dc-4492-aaf5-602aa717bd47/IMG_2865_large.jpg)
The kids really enjoyed this station (was voted most popular) since it allowed them to build and play a video game!
![Doh Controller](https://silvrback.s3.amazonaws.com/uploads/730d975e-6bf2-4b64-8f58-60e02e446d47/IMG_0670_large.jpg)
[game](https://scratch.mit.edu/projects/1758457/)

The next station was ran by [Evania Mans](https://www.linkedin.com/pub/evania-mans/41/a21/3b7).  This station involved taking the Makey Makey and creating a pair of bongo drums by jamming the alligator clips into the fruit.

##Banana Piano
The banana piano station was ran by [Mark Coleman - (Me)](http://www.kramnameloc.com).  This station allowed the kids to assemble a piano and use the bananas as keys on the keyboard.  Looking back at the station it was a bit of a challenge for the kids to open the clips to attach to the banana. It was actually easier to just jab them into the banana instead.
![Banana Panana](https://silvrback.s3.amazonaws.com/uploads/dcab077e-7231-4587-9989-eeeed7eab4a2/IMG_2863_large.jpg)
The fully functional banana piano is pictured below.  I could not be out done by the Mario Station which meant I loaded up Pac Man, however playing pac man with bananas is surprisingly difficult.

The last station was ran by [Rene Payo](https://www.linkedin.com/pub/renee-payo/97/655/46a) which had the kids build a piano style controller but it used flowers to activate the piano.
